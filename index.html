Information Design

Fractal Fantasy adopts a minimalist visual approach, prioritising clarity and user focus on audiovisual interaction. The interface is dominated by an ambient canvas that responds to mouse movement, while a compact control panel is positioned in the upper right corner. The panel features white text and purple sliders on a black background, creating strong visual contrast while drawing user attention. The control panel is vertically aligned, ensuring minimal intrusion on the main visual field. The limited colour palette – black, white, purple and various greys – reinforces cohesion and avoids distraction, aligning with the system’s purpose as an audiovisual synthesiser. The user is encouraged to explore the subtle changes without visual clutter, using proximity in the slider arrangement to suggest functional grouping. This visual restraint reflects the intention for deliberate, focused interaction.

In contrast, Limp Body Beat by Adult Swim uses a maximalist, surreal aesthetic to drive user interaction. The interface is saturated with pastel and fleshy tone, creating a textured, chaotic environment that mimics body parts in both form and colour. Instead of separating controls from the environment, interactive elements are embedded into the scene, some are visible in a grid, while others appear as a floating body part or buttons. Although the interface initially offers no textual guidance, this encourages a trial-and-error approach. The use of transparency in note blocks creates a slight visual hierarchy that helps navigation, though it’s not always intuitive. Unlike Fractal Fantasy’s reserved structure, Limp Body Beat thrives on sensory overload, with its abstract layering and textures designed to invoke curiosity and amusement rather than control and clarity.

Mapping

The mapping in Fractal Fantasy is deliberate and precise. Each slider within the control panel adjusts a specific audiovisual parameter, and changes are reflected in real time on the canvas. The layout is clean and consistent, with white slider knobs that are easily distinguishable, accompanied by numerical feedback to indicate exact values. This one-to-one relationship between control and effect supports the interaction design principle of predictability. As users adjust variables like “zoom”, “lightness” or “entropy”, the visual field and accompanying audio shift accordingly. The transparency between input and output is core to the interface’s experimental function; it is less about discovery through randomness and more about intentional modulation through control.

Limp Body Beat, by contrast, offers a much more physical and embodied mapping. While the beat-grid provides a consistent rhythm-based interface, sound can be manipulated by dragging and flinging a limp body into oncoming blocks. This introduces a layer of absurd physicality, where motion and timing directly influence audio output. Unlike Fractal Fantasy’s clearly labelled sliders, the controls here are less explicitly mapped, there are no labels or tooltips, but are instead designed to encourage exploration over precision. This supports the principle of learnability through play. The unpredictability of limb movements and their musical consequences reinforces the surreal tone, allowing chaotic experimentation rather than calculated control.

Characterisation

Characterisation plays a key role in setting expectations and guiding interaction in both interfaces. In Fractal Fantasy, the aesthetic of high-definition textures, glowing and subtle metallic ripples suggests a synthetic, immersive environment. This abstract environment cues users to expect ambient, non-linear interaction rather than functional or goal-oriented tasks. The minimalist design, combined with technical terminology in the control panel, frames the interface as a too or instrument for creative exploration. The experience presents itself as audiovisual art, and its interface characterises normal interaction as quiet, experimental, and deliberate.

In contrast, Limp Body Beat is immediately chaotic and surreal, evoking humour and absurdity through grotesque anatomy and pastel landscapes. The interface doesn’t resemble a traditional tool or app but instead a digital playable artwork. Users are thrown into an environment with minimal instruction, and the central figure – a limp, body part – suggests that this is not designed to be a serious system. Instead it characterises interaction as something to poke, drag and disrupt, with visual exaggeration reinforcing that unexpected results are normal. The aesthetic creates a context where unpredictability is expected and where formal feedback loops are secondary to playful discovery. The characterisation invites users to experiment and interpret the interface as fun sound instrument rather than a tool for precise creation.

Feedback

Fractal Fantasy provides immediate but subtle feedback to user input. Upon entering the interface, mouse movements cause changes in lighting and reflections on the canvas, accompanied by shifts in ambient sound. Adjusting any of the sliders in the control panel results in visual or audio alterations that reflect the new parameter values, though some feedback are not as easily perceptible. This creates a refined feedback system, cause and effect. It reinforces the interface’s ambient character and supports an experience of deep, intentional immersion rather than overstimulation. The consistency of feedback aligns well with users who seek controlled experimentation.

Limp Body Beat offers highly exaggerated and immediate feedback. Every beat that strikes the body triggers visual distortions and synchronised sound effects. The body flails, bends and reacts with comedic flair, making each action feel dynamic. Interacting with the beat-grid or changing tempo instantly affects the incoming note blocks, and the audio adjusts accordingly. This dramatic response supports visibility, as users can easily understand the results of their actions, encouraging repeat interaction due to the sense of fun and chaotic energy, rather than careful control.

Both UIs offer unique interaction designs, while serving different purposes. Fractal Fantasy is more controlled and minimalistic, and Limp Body Beat is more experimentative and humourous with its unpredictability. These contrasting approaches illustrate how interface aesthetics and interaction design principles can shape both expectations and behaviours of users within digital experiences.
